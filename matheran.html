Description

Matheran, an eco-sensitive hill station in the Western Ghats of Maharashtra, is known for its lush forests, breathtaking views, and colonial architecture. It is unique for being one of the few places in India where vehicles are not allowed, preserving its serene environment. Visitors can enjoy tranquil walks, horse rides, and explore various viewpoints offering stunning vistas of the surrounding landscapes.

How to Get There

Matheran is approximately 80 kilometers from Mumbai and around 120 kilometers from Pune. The hill station is accessible by road and train, followed by a toy train or a scenic trek.

  • By Air: The nearest airport is Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj International Airport in Mumbai, from where you can hire a taxi to reach Neral, the base town for Matheran.
  • By Rail: The nearest railway station is Neral Junction, which is well-connected to Mumbai and Pune. From Neral, you can take the toy train to Matheran or hire a taxi to Dasturi Point, followed by a short walk or horse ride.
  • By Road: Matheran is well-connected by road. You can drive or take a bus to Neral and then proceed to Dasturi Point. Private vehicles are not allowed beyond Dasturi Point.
