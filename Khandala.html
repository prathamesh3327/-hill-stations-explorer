Description

Khandala is a famous hill station located in the Western Ghats of Maharashtra. Known for its breathtaking views of valleys and lakes, Khandala is a favorite destination for picnickers, trekkers, and nature enthusiasts. The hill station offers pleasant weather throughout the year and is easily accessible from Mumbai and Pune.

How to Get There

Khandala is approximately 80 kilometers from Mumbai and around 70 kilometers from Pune. The hill station is well-connected by road and can be reached by car or bus from both cities. The Mumbai-Pune Expressway (NH-48) offers a scenic drive to Khandala, with numerous viewpoints along the way.

  • By Air: The nearest airport is Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj International Airport in Mumbai, from where you can hire a taxi or take a bus to Khandala.
  • By Rail: Khandala has its own railway station, which is a major stop on the Mumbai-Pune route. Several express and local trains connect Khandala to Mumbai and Pune.
  • By Road: Khandala is well-connected by road and can be accessed via the Mumbai-Pune Expressway (NH-48) and the Khandala-Lonavala Road.
