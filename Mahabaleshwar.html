Description

Mahabaleshwar is one of the most famous hill stations in India, located in the Western Ghats of Maharashtra. Known for its stunning viewpoints, strawberry farms, and ancient temples, Mahabaleshwar offers a perfect blend of natural beauty and cultural heritage. The hill station's pleasant climate, lush greenery, and numerous attractions make it a popular destination for tourists year-round.

How to Get There

Mahabaleshwar is approximately 120 kilometers from Pune and around 260 kilometers from Mumbai. The hill station is well-connected by road and can be easily reached by car or bus from both cities. The route to Mahabaleshwar is scenic, with winding roads and beautiful landscapes.

  • By Air: The nearest airport is Pune International Airport, from where you can hire a taxi or take a bus to Mahabaleshwar.
  • By Rail: The nearest railway station is Wathar, around 60 kilometers away. Pune Railway Station is another major railway station, about 120 kilometers from Mahabaleshwar. From there, you can hire a taxi or take a bus to Mahabaleshwar.
  • By Road: Mahabaleshwar is well-connected by road and can be accessed via the Mumbai-Pune Expressway (NH-48) and then the Mahabaleshwar Road.
