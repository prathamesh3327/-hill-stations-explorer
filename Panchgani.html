Description

Panchgani, a picturesque hill station in the Western Ghats of Maharashtra, is known for its panoramic views, strawberry farms, and colonial-era buildings. It offers a serene and rejuvenating environment, making it an ideal destination for a relaxing holiday. The hill station is also famous for its educational institutions and the Table Land, a vast expanse of flat laterite rock.

How to Get There

Panchgani is approximately 100 kilometers from Pune and around 250 kilometers from Mumbai. The hill station is well-connected by road and can be easily reached by car or bus from both cities.

  • By Air: The nearest airport is Pune International Airport, from where you can hire a taxi or take a bus to Panchgani.
  • By Rail: The nearest railway station is Wathar, around 45 kilometers away. Pune Railway Station is another major railway station, about 100 kilometers from Panchgani. From there, you can hire a taxi or take a bus to Panchgani.
  • By Road: Panchgani is well-connected by road and can be accessed via the Mumbai-Pune Expressway (NH-48) and then the Panchgani-Mahabaleshwar Road.
